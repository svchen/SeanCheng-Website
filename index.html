Hello, my name is Sean Cheng, I am a recent graduate from MiraCosta Community College class of 2019, now I am a Business Administration undergraduate at the University of California, Riverside. My concentration will be in Accounting because I plan on taking the Certified Public Accountant exam after I graduate. After taking my CPA exam, I plan to work for an accounting firm to accumulate more experience and gain a wider network. I plan on continuing my education to earn an MBA with a focus in management. With the MBA, I want to work as a cost accountant or a budget analyst for a private firm. However, I am also considering opening a small accounting firm or freelancing as a tax accountant.

A fun fact that people seem to always get excited about is that I worked for my parents in a donut shop since the age of nine. My family migrated to the United States of America when I was seven and two years later with the help of my uncle, we were able to open up a donut shop, Oceanside Donuts, in Oceanside, California. I currently commute back to Oceanside every Thursday to help my parents run the shop. Prior to college, I spent nearly every single day helping them out by restocking drinks, taking out the trash, evaluating inventory, and being a cashier. During the summer of 2018, I applied and obtained a job at Frazier Farms as a courtesy clerk. This was my first experience working for someone other than my parents. The job was only temporary-lasting for about three months-but it allowed for me to translate what I knew from working for my parents to a more real workplace environment. Unfortunately, I had to quit after three months on the job because it was interfering with my education.

During my free time I like to spend time with my friend, this usually comes in the form of videos games and soccer. I also enjoy going on hikes through natural parks and beaches to relax my mind. I had actually wanted a zoologist ever since I was nine, however, through my own failure, I was unable to enter the biology program of any school. While this experience was saddening, it taught me a valuable lesson: failure does not lead to success, unless corrected. Now, I find more motivated then ever to succeed and be the second in my family to obtain a college degree.
